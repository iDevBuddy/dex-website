## How does Square let a restaurant take orders from ChatGPT and Claude?
Square released a dedicated ChatGPT app and a Claude plugin that expose a merchant’s Square Online Ordering catalog to the AI chat interface. When a user asks the assistant for a nearby restaurant, the AI can display real‑time menu items, modifiers, and stock levels, let the user add items to a basket, and complete checkout either inside the chat window or on a pre‑filled Square ordering page. The order lands in the same Square POS/KDS the restaurant already uses and is tagged as “AI integration” for reporting.

## What fees will a restaurant actually pay?
| Plan | Transaction fee | Flat courier fee |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| Square Plus | 3.3% + $0.30 per order | $7‑$10 per delivery |
| Square Premium | 2.9% + $0.30 per order | $7‑$10 per delivery |

There are **no marketplace commissions** (the 15‑30% typical on DoorDash, Uber Eats, Grubhub). Restaurants can absorb the flat courier fee or pass it to customers.

## Why is the integration “zero‑friction” for restaurants?
1. **No development required** – a merchant only needs an active Square Online Ordering profile; the plugins read the live catalog via Square’s backend.
2. **Dynamic inventory sync** – pricing, modifiers, and stock are refreshed in real time, preventing unavailable items from being offered.
3. **Existing workflow** – orders appear in the familiar POS/KDS, requiring no new staff training.
4. **Auditability** – the Order by Cash App plugin in ChatGPT or Claude’s extension directory lets operators view every AI‑originated order.

## Business impact
- **Margin protection** – eliminating 15‑30% aggregator cuts means each digital order retains the full transaction amount minus Square’s modest fee and the flat courier charge.
- **Operational simplicity** – the integration plugs into the Square Dashboard and POS already in use; no separate tablet or nightly data imports are needed.
- **Customer experience** – users can order while chatting with their preferred AI, keeping the purchase flow within a single conversational window.
- **Limited discovery** – AI agents surface restaurants only when users ask; Square does not provide paid placement or large‑scale marketing, so merchants must drive AI‑driven traffic themselves (e.g., QR‑linked prompts, social media).

## Quick setup guide
1. **Activate Square Online Ordering** – ensure menu, modifiers, and inventory are up‑to‑date in the Square Dashboard.
2. **Choose a plan** – Square Plus or Premium (the latter offers the lower 2.9% + $0.30 fee).
3. **Install the ChatGPT app** – locate “Square” in the ChatGPT app store and complete the one‑click OAuth flow.
4. **Install the Claude plugin** – find the Square plugin in Claude’s extension directory and authorize the same Square account.
5. **Test the flow** – ask the AI “What’s good for lunch near me?” and place a test order; confirm it appears in your POS.
6. **Configure delivery** – select Square’s white‑label dispatch network, set your flat courier fee range, and decide who bears the fee.
7. **Monitor performance** – use Square’s reporting to track “AI integration” orders, compare margins, and adjust pricing or delivery fees as needed.

## Contrarian take
Square removes percentage‑based commissions, but it does **not** subsidize delivery logistics. The flat $7‑$10 courier fee still impacts margins on low‑ticket orders, and the lack of built‑in marketing means AI‑driven discovery may generate fewer orders than aggregator platforms unless the restaurant invests in external promotion. In short, the model trades high‑commission risk for low‑visibility risk.

## Risks and caveats
- **Geography** – only U.S. Food & Beverage sellers with an active Square Online Ordering profile are eligible.
- **Delivery logistics** – Square’s white‑label network provides a flat fee but no marketing reach; merchants may need additional strategies to fill delivery capacity.
- **Discovery limits** – AI agents surface restaurants based on user queries, not paid placement, which can slow customer acquisition compared with aggregators.

## What to do next
If you already run Square POS, enable the ChatGPT and Claude plugins this week and pilot a single menu segment. Track cost per order against your current aggregator spend. If the margin improvement outweighs the flat delivery fee and you can generate enough AI‑driven traffic, expand the integration across your full menu.

### Bottom line
Square’s AI plugins provide a **low‑cost, plug‑and‑play channel** for direct orders from ChatGPT and Claude, preserving margins that traditional delivery apps erode. The trade‑off is reduced discovery power and the need to handle flat delivery fees yourself. For independent restaurants already on Square, the integration is a practical way to capture more profit on digital orders.
