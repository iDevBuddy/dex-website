## How can a restaurant take orders from ChatGPT or Claude?

Square released a **ChatGPT app** and a **Claude plugin** that expose a restaurant’s Square catalog inside the LLM. When a user asks the model to order, the AI pulls live menu data, builds a basket, and either completes checkout inside the chat window or redirects the shopper to the merchant’s online‑ordering page with the cart pre‑filled. No custom code, webhook, or separate API is required—just an active Square Online Ordering profile.

## What fees actually apply?

| Fee type | Square (standard) | DoorDash / Uber Eats / Grubhub |
|----------|-------------------|--------------------------------|
| Transaction fee | 3.3% + $0.30 (Plus) or 2.9% + $0.30 (Premium) | 15‑30% of order total |
| Delivery charge | Flat $7‑$10 courier fee (white‑label) | Percentage‑based commission (often 15‑30%) |
| Setup cost | $0 – live on launch | Variable onboarding fees, often hidden |

The flat courier fee can be absorbed by the restaurant or passed to the customer, preserving food margins because Square never takes a percentage of the delivery charge.

## How does this affect restaurant margins?

Aggregator commissions can erode profitability on digital orders. By routing orders through Square’s LLM channel, restaurants keep the full order value and only pay the standard online‑ordering fee. The only additional cost is the flat delivery fee, which is fully controllable.

## What steps are required to launch?
1. **Activate Square Online Ordering** – must be a U.S. Food & Beverage seller.
2. **Enable the ChatGPT app** in the Square App Marketplace **or** add the Claude plugin via the Claude extension directory.
3. **Confirm catalog accuracy** – items, modifiers, and stock are synced in real time.
4. **Set delivery preferences** – decide whether to absorb the $7‑$10 fee or charge it to the customer.
5. **Train staff** – orders appear in the Square POS and on existing Kitchen Display Systems.
6. **Monitor performance** – AI‑origin orders are tagged in Square’s backend reporting for margin and volume analysis.

## Risks and caveats
- **Delivery network coverage** – The white‑label courier service may not reach every neighborhood or match the speed of major aggregators.
- **Visibility is query‑driven** – Orders depend on AI agents surfacing the restaurant; a clean, keyword‑rich catalog is essential.
- **Standard fees still apply** – For high‑ticket items, the 3.3% + $0.30 fee can be higher than the lowest card‑processor rates.
- **U.S. only** – The integration is currently limited to U.S. merchants with an active Square Online Ordering profile.

## Contrarian take
Most coverage celebrates the novelty of AI ordering. The real disruption is **margin reclamation**: Square eliminates the 15‑30% aggregator cut while still providing AI‑driven discovery. Restaurants gain a low‑cost acquisition channel without surrendering profit to percentage‑based delivery fees.
