## What the engine does

An AI authority blog engine is not just a writing script. It is a publishing system with controls.

- It discovers topics from market signals, customer questions, Slack commands, and editorial ideas
- It scores topics before spending time on a draft
- It produces a research brief with intent, reader pain points, examples, sources, and internal link ideas
- It drafts content with a specific business outcome in mind
- It creates image prompts, listenable audio scripts, FAQ blocks, and schema data
- It blocks publication when quality or SEO checks fail
- It syncs status to Slack and Notion so the workflow is visible

## Why quality gates matter

AI makes it easy to publish too much. That is also the risk.

The safest version of this system is aggressive about research and conservative about publishing. A post should not go live just because a model can write it. It should go live because it answers a real question, gives practical steps, cites useful references, and connects naturally to your business expertise.

## A practical workflow

Start with a repeatable pipeline:

- Discover topics from Google News RSS, Reddit, Hacker News, GitHub activity, Notion ideas, and Slack requests
- Score each topic for business value, search intent, topical authority, and monetization potential
- Build a research brief before drafting
- Generate a structured article with examples, mistakes, FAQs, sources, and a clear next step
- Run SEO optimization for title, meta description, slug, headings, snippets, schema, and internal links
- Generate or request a featured image from an AI image provider
- Generate an audio script and use browser text-to-speech until local TTS is configured
- Send the draft to Slack and Notion for approval
- Publish through GitHub only after quality checks pass

## What to avoid

Do not automate tactics that create risk instead of authority.

- Do not copy competitor articles
- Do not spin existing content
- Do not create fake authors or fake expertise
- Do not publish dozens of weak pages
- Do not stuff keywords into headings
- Do not build doorway pages
- Do not hide text or show different content to crawlers

## What to do next

The best MVP is a system that publishes fewer, stronger posts.

For the first two days, focus on the working foundation: a clean blog, crawlable article pages, metadata, RSS, sitemap, a listen button, a draft generator, a quality checker, Slack notifications, Notion sync, and GitHub Actions. Advanced monitoring and refresh automation can come after the first useful publishing loop is stable.
